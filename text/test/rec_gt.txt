crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_1.jpg	Definition: - A Psychological test is a standardized measure of a sample of a person's
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_2.jpg	behaviour that is used to measure the individual differences that exist among people. A
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_3.jpg	psychological test is an objective and standardized measure of an individual's mental and/or
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_4.jpg	behavioural characteristics. A Psychological test is a systematic procedure or observing
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_5.jpg	person's behaviour or performing, describing it with the aid of a numerical scale or category
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_6.jpg	system. Mostly tests are used as a way of measuring differences between people or
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_7.jpg	differences in the same person over time. Psychological tests are written, visual, or verbal
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_8.jpg	evaluations administered to assess the cognitive and emotional functioning of children and
crop_img/MA-S-IV-Guidence-1_page-0001_crop_9.jpg	adults.
